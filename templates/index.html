Why Focus on Child Neglect?

Child neglect is one of the most common forms of child maltreatment, yet it often goes unnoticed and unreported. It occurs when a child's basic needs‚Äîsuch as food, shelter, education, medical care, and emotional support‚Äîare not met, leading to serious physical, psychological, and developmental harm.

Key Statistics:

  • Neglect accounts for over 60% of child abuse cases globally
  • Children who experience neglect are at higher risk for mental health issues, substance abuse, and poor academic performance
  • Early detection and intervention can significantly improve outcomes for affected children

Our Mission: This platform leverages machine learning and data analytics to identify patterns and risk factors associated with child neglect. By analyzing crime statistics, demographic data, and historical trends, we aim to support policymakers, social workers, and child protection agencies in making informed decisions and allocating resources effectively.
